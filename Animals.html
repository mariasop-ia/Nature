Giant Cloud Rat

The Northern Luzon Giant Cloud Rat, also known as the Northern Luzon Cloud Rat or simply Cloud Rat, is a remarkable rodent species endemic to the forests of Northern Luzon in the Philippines. It's characterized by its large size, dense fur, and long, bushy tail, making it one of the largest rodents in the world. Despite its size, it's elusive and primarily nocturnal, feeding on a diet of fruits, leaves, and other plant material.

Phloeomys pallidus

Forest Mouse

The Northern Luzon Forest Mouse, also known as Apomys microdon, is a small rodent species found in the forests of Northern Luzon in the Philippines. It is characterized by its brown fur and relatively small size compared to other rodents in the region. As an important part of the forest ecosystem, it plays a role in seed dispersal and maintaining biodiversity.

Apomys abrae

Banded Flowerpecker

The Banded Flowerpecker, scientifically known as Dicaeum haematostictum, is a charming little bird found in Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It gets its name from the distinct dark band that runs across its chest. These tiny birds, usually measuring around 8-9 centimeters in length, primarily feed on nectar, fruits, and small insects, making them important pollinators and contributors to the ecosystem. They are often spotted in dense forest habitats, where they flit among the foliage in search of food.

Dicaeum haematostictum

Hornbill

In Benguet, a province in the Cordillera Administrative Region of the Philippines, one of the notable hornbill species is the Luzon Hornbill (Penelopides manillae). This striking bird is recognized for its large size, black plumage, and distinctive casque atop its bill. While it once inhabited various forested areas across Luzon, including Benguet, habitat loss and fragmentation have made sightings less common. Conservation efforts are underway to protect remaining populations and their habitats in the region.

Penelopides manillae

Warty Pig

The Philippine warty pig (Sus philippensis) is a wild pig species endemic to the Philippines. It is known for its distinctive appearance, featuring prominent warts on its face and a bristly coat. These pigs inhabit various forested habitats across the Philippine archipelago and are primarily herbivorous, feeding on roots, fruits, and other vegetation. Due to habitat loss and hunting, the Philippine warty pig is considered vulnerable to extinction, with conservation efforts focused on protecting remaining populations and their habitats.

Sus philippensis

Leaf-nosed Bat

The Baguio Leaf-nosed Bat, scientifically known as Hipposideros emiliae, is a rare species endemic to the montane forests of Baguio and nearby regions in the Philippines. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf-shaped nose, which aids in echolocation and foraging for insects in its forest habitat. Due to habitat loss and disturbance, this unique bat species is considered vulnerable, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its natural environment and ensure its survival.

Hipposideros emiliae

Bleeding-heart Dove

The Luzon Bleeding-heart Dove, scientifically known as Gallicolumba luzonica, is a species of dove endemic to the Philippines, particularly found in the forests of Luzon, including areas around Baguio. This bird is named for the distinctive red patch on its breast, which resembles a bleeding wound. The species is known for its shy and elusive nature, often hiding in dense foliage and only emerging to forage for seeds and fruits on the forest floor. Due to habitat loss and hunting pressures, the Luzon Bleeding-heart Dove is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect its remaining forest habitat and ensure its long-term survival in the wild.

Gallicolumba luzonica

Striped Grass Mouse

The Baguio Striped Grass Mouse, scientifically known as Mus caroli, is a small rodent species endemic to the mountainous regions of Baguio and nearby areas in the Cordillera region of the Philippines. This mouse is characterized by its distinctive striped fur pattern, which helps it blend into its grassland habitat. As an herbivore, it primarily feeds on grasses, seeds, and other plant material found in its environment. The Baguio Striped Grass Mouse plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by serving as prey for predators and contributing to seed dispersal. However, like many other small mammal species, it faces threats from habitat loss and fragmentation due to human activities. Conservation efforts are essential to safeguard the natural habitats of the Baguio Striped Grass Mouse and preserve its role in the local ecosystem.

Mus caroli

Forest Skink

The Baguio Forest Skink, scientifically known as Sphenomorphus baguioensis, is a species of lizard endemic to the mountainous forests of Baguio and nearby areas in the Cordillera region of the Philippines. This small reptile is characterized by its sleek body and distinct coloration, often featuring shades of brown or gray with darker markings. As an inhabitant of montane forests, the Baguio Forest Skink plays a crucial role in its ecosystem, contributing to insect control and serving as prey for larger predators. However, due to habitat loss and fragmentation, this species faces threats to its survival, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to preserve the biodiversity of Baguio's forests.

Sphenomorphus baguioensis

Philippine Eagle

The Philippine Eagle, scientifically known as Pithecophaga jefferyi, is one of the world's largest and most powerful eagles, endemic to the Philippines and occasionally spotted in forested areas near Baguio. With its distinctive crest and massive wingspan, it's an apex predator known for hunting a variety of prey, including monkeys and other small mammals. Unfortunately, habitat loss and hunting have made it critically endangered, necessitating concerted conservation efforts to safeguard its remaining populations and the ecosystems they inhabit.

Pithecophaga jefferyi
