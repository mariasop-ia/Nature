Mount Pulag

Mount Pulag is the highest peak in Luzon and the third-highest mountain in the Philippines. It is renowned for its majestic sea of clouds, grassland slopes, and diverse ecosystems, including mossy forests and dwarf bamboo groves. It is a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Amburayan River

The Amburayan River is a major river system that flows through Benguet and nearby provinces. It originates from the Cordillera mountains and meanders through valleys and gorges, providing water for irrigation, hydroelectric power generation, and recreational activities like river rafting and fishing.

Amburayan River

Sagada Cave

The Sagada Cave System is a network of limestone caves located in the municipality of Sagada, which is part of Benguet's neighboring province, Mountain Province. These caves, such as Sumaguing Cave and Lumiang Cave, feature stunning rock formations, underground rivers, and chambers filled with stalactites and stalagmites. They are popular destinations for spelunking and cave exploration.

Mount Ulap

Mount Ulap, nestled in Itogon, Benguet, is a captivating eco-trail renowned for its stunning vistas of the Cordillera mountain range. Hikers traverse through diverse ecosystems, including pine forests and grasslands, encountering endemic flora and fauna along the way. At the summit, breathtaking panoramic views await, making Mount Ulap a cherished destination for nature enthusiasts seeking adventure and tranquility amidst the clouds.

Mount Ulap

Mount Ugo

Mount Ugo is a prominent mountain located on the border of Benguet and Nueva Vizcaya provinces. It is known for its scenic trails, dense pine forests, and panoramic views of surrounding mountains and valleys. It is a favorite hiking destination, offering both challenging climbs and opportunities to appreciate nature's beauty.

Mount Ugo

Bomod-Ok Falls

Bomod-Ok Falls is a breathtaking waterfall located in the municipality of Sagada, Mountain Province, near the border with Benguet. It cascades from a towering height into a cool pool below, surrounded by verdant vegetation and limestone cliffs. It is accessible via a scenic trek through terraced rice fields and scenic landscapes.

Bomod-Ok Falls

Mount Santo Tomas

Mount Santo Tomas, located near Baguio City in the province of Benguet, is one of the prominent peaks in the Cordillera mountain range. Offering sweeping views of Baguio City and surrounding areas, it stands as a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts. Covered in lush greenery, the mountain provides a serene retreat from urban life, inviting visitors to explore its trails and appreciate the natural beauty of the region.

Santo Tomas

Ambuklao Dam

Ambuklao Dam, situated in the province of Benguet, is a vital hydroelectric facility that harnesses the power of the Agno River to generate electricity. With its towering structure and vast reservoir, it serves as both a source of renewable energy and a scenic attraction. The dam's serene surroundings, framed by verdant hills and crystalline waters, offer a peaceful retreat for visitors seeking respite from the hustle and bustle of city life. Additionally, the dam provides opportunities for recreational activities such as boating, fishing, and picnicking, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike.

Ambuklao

Bridal Veil Falls

Bridal Veil Falls, nestled in Tuba, Benguet, is a mesmerizing cascade named for its ethereal resemblance to a bride's veil. Cascading gracefully down rugged rock formations, it creates a picturesque scene surrounded by lush greenery. The falls offer a tranquil atmosphere, inviting visitors to relax and immerse themselves in the soothing sounds of nature. Accessible via a short hike, Bridal Veil Falls is a beloved destination for nature lovers and photographers seeking the beauty and serenity of the Cordillera mountains

Mount Timbak

Mount Timbak, nestled in the Cordillera mountain range in the province of Benguet, is a revered destination for hikers and cultural enthusiasts alike. Standing as the third highest peak in Luzon, it offers panoramic views of neighboring peaks, verdant valleys, and terraced rice fields below. Beyond its natural beauty, Mount Timbak is also home to the "Mossy Forest," a unique ecosystem rich in biodiversity, including endemic plant species. Additionally, the mountain is dotted with ancient burial sites known as "Applai," offering insights into the indigenous culture and traditions of the Cordillera people. As a sacred mountain and a haven for outdoor exploration, Mount Timbak beckons adventurers to discover its scenic wonders and cultural treasures.

Mount Timbak

Agno River

The Agno River, meandering through the provinces of Benguet, Pangasinan, and Nueva Vizcaya, is one of Luzon's longest rivers, serving as a lifeline for communities and ecosystems. Its crystal-clear waters support diverse aquatic life and provide essential irrigation for agriculture. The river's scenic beauty, framed by lush forests and rolling hills, offers a tranquil backdrop for activities like rafting and kayaking, attracting adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts alike. As a vital natural resource and a source of recreation, the Agno River holds both ecological and cultural significance for the regions it traverses.
